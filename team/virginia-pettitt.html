Africa Was Born in Her

Virginia Pettitt wasn't born in Africa — but as she often says, Africa was born in her. Half Zimbabwean, she grew up with the continent woven into her identity, her family stories, and her sense of belonging.

That connection shaped everything that followed. From building businesses that celebrate Black beauty to creating content that showcases African luxury, Virginia has spent her career ensuring that people who look like her see themselves reflected in the spaces they deserve to occupy.

In 2020, she opened The Curl Bar London — a salon and safe space for natural hair in Crouch End. It proved she could build physical businesses rooted in community and cultural pride. But it was her travels across Africa that revealed the next opportunity.

Visiting luxury properties across the continent, Virginia noticed a pattern: world-class experiences being marketed with imagery that excluded the very audiences most eager to book them. The diaspora was searching for connection. The properties were failing to speak to them. NUBEAUX was born to bridge that gap — combining Virginia's understanding of both sides to create something the industry has been missing.
